Programme Overview

The B. Tech. Petroleum Engineering Program at MIT-WPU is based on three cardinal principles: providing social context to education, giving maximum choice to the student to choose a career path and encouraging experiential learning. To that effect, a B.Tech in Petroleum engineering can be broadly divided into 3 district subcategories.

Operations Management:

A multi-disciplinary field that focuses on managing all aspects of an organization’s operations to provide products and services. In Operation management, an individual will learn how to increase productivity and reduce costs, improve flexibility to meet rapidly changing customer needs, enhance product quality and improve customer service.

Exploration and Production:

These are deemed to fall within the upstream sector of the oil and gas industry. Petroleum Exploration and essential engineering for Production of hydrocarbons are the oil and gas industry’s two main subsurface disciplines, which focus on maximizing economic recovery of hydrocarbons from subsurface reservoirs.

Petroleum Geology and Geophysics:

This focuses on provision of a static description of the hydrocarbon reservoir rock, while Petroleum Engineering focuses on estimation of the recoverable volume of this resource using a detailed understanding of the physical and chemical behavior of oil, water and gas within porous rock which may be at very high depth, pressure, and temperature conditions.
