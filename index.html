Drilling and Hoisting

Drilling:

Drilling is a process whereby a hole is bored using a drill bit to create a well for oil and natural gas production. The Oilfield Drilling process helps in exploring and reaching out natural resources such as petroleum, brine, and natural gas. Drilling is an initial phase of oil and gas production. A well is drilled by using either manual or mechanical drilling equipment that helps in drilling a hole in the earth's surface, just above natural oil or gas resources.
In our model , we have used motor with specification “single phase AC synchronous motor; 240V-50HZ; 60rpm; 3Kg-cm “ in order to rotate drill string and ultimately drill bit, to drill hole.
Drill pipe : 1 ft long with 1” OD , coupler 1” ID

Hoisting

Hoisting System is the system used on a drilling rig to perform all lifting activities on the rig. These activities basically include the lowering and lifting of necessary equipment in or out of the hole. This system is the main component that performs the drilling operation by either lifting or lowering the casing or drill pipes to drill and finally complete the well.
The Hoisting System consists of three major components:

  • Derrick: This is a long steel tower used in the drilling rig to provide vertical height which is required to raise or lower sections of pipes into the hole. It is the most important identifiable symbol on the drilling rig.
  • Block & Tackle System: This is a system in the Hoisting System that comprises of travelling block, crown block and the drilling line. They are used to provide a mechanical advantage that helps in handling large loads easily.
  • Drawworks: The main operating component of the Hoisting System. They are used to transmit power from prime movers to the hoisting drum that lifts the casing, drilling string or tubing string. They also transmit power to a rotary drive sprocket that helps in driving a rotary table.

In our model, we have used:

  • Derrick: 71” (long).
  • Draw work motor (specification) : single phase AC synchronous motor; 240V-50HZ; 60rpm; 3Kg-cm
  • Drill line : 500” (in length)
  • crown block: 3 pulleys with diameter 2”
  • travelling block:2 pulleys with diameter 4”

Circulation and Cementation

Cementation is a part of the process of preparing a well for further drilling, production or abandonment, cementing a well is the procedure of developing and pumping cement into place in a wellbore.
Circulation is the complete,circuitous part that the drilling fluid travels. As shown in our model, starting from the main pump, the mud travels through the stand pipe, the Kelly, drill pipe ,drill collar and comes out from the nozzles in the drill bit.
This mud lifts the cuttings of the drilled portion and travels upward through the annulus between drill pipe and casing. We have used various mud conditioning equipments to clean and treat the mud coming from the annulus. This includes:

  1. Shale Shaker-which removes large solids ( cuttings ) from the drilling mud.
  2. Desander-for removing sand that accompanies the drilling mud.
  3. Desilter-to separate the silt.
The treated mud finally goes into the initial mud pit.
